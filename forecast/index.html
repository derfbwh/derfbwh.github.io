Allan Lichtman's 13 Keys to the White House (Midterm Adaptation)

We adapt the historic 13 keys to determine the midterm political landscape. When the incumbent party (Republicans in 2026) has 6 or more false keys, the challenging party is favored to win the midterms.

Key Metric Status Key Definition & 2026 Context
1. Party Mandate FALSE After the 2024 elections, Republicans held a slim majority. A highly fractionalized House makes this key False.
2. Incumbent Nomination TRUE No major challenger to Republican leadership in Senate primary structures.
3. Incumbency TRUE Incumbent President is a Republican, giving the GOP leadership structure the incumbency key.
4. Third Party TRUE No significant third-party challenge in congressional midterm contests.
5. Short-Term Economy TRUE The US economy is not currently in a recession during the election year campaign.
6. Long-Term Economy FALSE Real per capita economic growth is lagging compared to the previous two presidential terms.
7. Policy Change TRUE Major domestic policy bills and deregulation changes enacted in the 119th Congress.
8. Social Unrest TRUE No sustained national social unrest disrupting governance in early 2026.
9. Scandal FALSE Ongoing congressional ethics investigations and high-profile administrative controversies.
10. Foreign Failures TRUE No massive, catastrophic foreign military disasters occurring in early 2026.
11. Foreign Successes FALSE No landmark global treaties or major, highly visible international achievements.
12. Incumbent Charisma FALSE The current executive leadership lacks the sweeping cross-partisan appeal of a national hero.
13. Challenger Charisma TRUE The leading Democratic congressional campaigns do not feature any singular charismatic national star.
